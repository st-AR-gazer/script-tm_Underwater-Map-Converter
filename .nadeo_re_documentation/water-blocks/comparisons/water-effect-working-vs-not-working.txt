Water-on-car working vs not working
===================================

Source of truth:
- user manual in-game testing
- test maps built specifically to check whether blocks apply the water effect to the car

Interpretation:
- "Working" means the block applies the expected water-on-car / wet-wheels behavior.
- "Not working" means the block can visually carry water or is water-adjacent, but does not apply the water effect to the car.

-------

Working:
- RoadTechDiagLeftStraightOnBeach
- RoadTechDiagRightStraightOnBeach
- RoadDirtDiagLeftStraightOnBeach
- RoadDirtDiagRightStraightOnBeach
- RoadBumpDiagLeftStraightOnBeach
- RoadBumpDiagRightStraightOnBeach
- RoadIceWithWallLeftDiagLeftStraightOnBeach
- RoadIceWithWallRightDiagLeftStraightOnBeach
- RoadIceWithWallLeftDiagRightStraightOnBeach
- RoadIceWithWallRightDiagRightStraightOnBeach

Not working:
- everything else in the tested `*OnBeach*` / `*OnSeaCliff13*` shoreline families

Current technical explanation
-----------------------------

BlueBay and WhiteShore:
- The current strongest structural predictor is whether the block's top-level prefab directly includes the local zone water base prefab.
- In both environments, the blocks that worked in testing align with that pattern, and the blocks that did not work mostly align with the absence of that direct water-base inclusion.

GreenCoast and RedIsland:
- User testing indicates a broader working set than BlueBay / WhiteShore.
- This means the top-level water-base check is not sufficient to explain those environments completely.
- The most likely explanation is that GreenCoast / RedIsland bridge/shore transition prefabs or other deeper local prefabs are enough to carry the gameplay water semantics even when the top-level prefab does not directly list the local zone water base.

Bottom line:
- BlueBay / WhiteShore currently look strict.
- GreenCoast / RedIsland currently look broader.
